Auto-tuninig charging rate for lithium ion battery.

Conditioning and controlling battery charge/discharge is predominant in a battery application. Problems of charging speed affect battery lifetime and desired practical usage. Capacity degradation and safety issue are the main concerns for fast charging. Fundamentally, overpotential during the charging process leads to lithium dendrite formation, which further produces overheat, capacity degradation, and separator piercing. Therefore, precise predictions of the state of charge (SOC) and controlling charging current density become critical studies for efficient battery usage. For the given battery chemistry, the lifetime degradation linearly decreases with the capacity under the same charging/discharging rate, e.g., 0.05C. With the curve, we can estimate the cycle number and, therefore, the battery's full capacity at a particular time. By incorporating Coulombic efficiency (CE), we can determine the charging capacity or SOC at a specific charging current density.
